I am a first-year PhD student in Supply Chain and Information Systems and a Dual-Title PhD student in Operations Research at the Pennsylvania State University, advised by Prof. Saurabh Bansal. I received my Master’s degree in Agricultural & Applied Economics from the University of Wisconsin–Madison.

My research lies at the intersection of supply chain management and applied economics. I have studied agricultural supply chain optimization and firms’ adaptation to natural disasters. More broadly, I focus on supply chain resilience and the coordination of agri-food supply chains.

I bring strong technical expertise to my work, with proficiency in Stata, R, Python, and LaTeX, as well as experience with high-performance computing for large-scale data analysis. I collaborate with mentors and institutions in both the U.S. and China on projects linking supply chains and rural development, and I actively partner with firms to solve real-world supply chain optimization problems, bridging theory and practice to generate actionable insights.
